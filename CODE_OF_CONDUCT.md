# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We — contributors, maintainers, and operators of LITNUP — pledge to make participation in this project a productive, professional, and harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity, level of experience, nationality, race, religion, sexual orientation, or any other dimension of personhood.

This document sets the floor, not the ceiling. We expect contributors to treat each other with substantially more grace than this document strictly requires.

---

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.** Disagreement is fine. Contempt is not.
- **Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.** Especially of your own work.
- **Focusing on what is best for the protocol.** Not your ego, not your faction, not your bag.
- **Showing empathy for other community members.** People are not their worst day.
- **Crediting prior work + collaborators.** Visibly, not grudgingly.
- **Disagreeing in public, dunking in private.** (Or better: not dunking at all.)

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment in any form — public or private, sustained or one-shot
- Personal attacks, doxxing, or threats
- Trolling or deliberately bad-faith engagement
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of governance / technical discussions
- Sexualized language or imagery; unwelcome sexual attention
- Promotion of products, tokens, or services unrelated to LITNUP in protocol-owned channels
- Coordinated brigading or "raids" on other projects from LITNUP-affiliated accounts
- Pretending to represent LITNUP when you don't

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:

- Within all project spaces (GitHub, Forum, Discord, Twitter/X under official handles, video calls, conferences attended on behalf of the foundation)
- When representing LITNUP in any public context

It does NOT apply to:

- Personal conduct unrelated to LITNUP
- Other projects' communities (we ask you to respect their codes of conduct, not enforce ours)

## Enforcement

### Reporting

Conduct violations may be reported to `conduct@litnup.xyz`. Reports are reviewed by the foundation's Conduct Committee (3 people; rotating annually).

We strongly prefer reports include:
- What happened
- When and where
- Who was involved
- Any evidence (screenshots, transcripts, recordings) you're comfortable sharing
- What outcome you're hoping for

All reports are confidential. The reporter's identity is shared only with the Conduct Committee unless the reporter explicitly authorizes broader disclosure.

### Response

The Conduct Committee will:
1. Acknowledge the report within 48 hours
2. Investigate within 7 days
3. Take action proportional to the violation
4. Communicate the outcome to the reporter

### Actions

Depending on severity, actions may include:

- **Private clarification.** First-time, minor violations get a private heads-up.
- **Public warning.** Repeated or moderate violations get a public note in the relevant channel.
- **Temporary suspension.** Serious violations may result in temporary ban from project spaces (7-90 days).
- **Permanent ban.** Severe or repeated violations may result in permanent removal from all project spaces.
- **Legal action.** Threats, doxxing, harassment, or fraud may be referred to law enforcement.

### Appeal

Anyone who receives an enforcement action may appeal to the Foundation Board within 30 days. Appeals are reviewed by board members who were not part of the original Conduct Committee decision.

## Conduct Committee

Names + handles of current committee members are published on `/about`. Terms are 1 year; rotation is annual.

Committee members:
- Must be foundation board members or trusted community members
- Cannot serve more than 2 consecutive years
- Must recuse from any case involving a conflict of interest
- Are subject to this Code of Conduct themselves

## What this Code is NOT

To preempt common misunderstandings:

### It is NOT a tool for silencing disagreement

You can disagree, push back, and argue for your position. You cannot make it personal, demean others, or sustain bad-faith argument tactics. The line is between "I think your proposal is wrong" (fine) and "you're an idiot for proposing this" (not fine).

### It is NOT a substitute for technical or governance debate

Bad technical decisions get reversed through governance and code review. The Conduct Committee does not adjudicate technical disputes.

### It is NOT a way to win arguments

If you report someone for code-of-conduct violation as a tactic to win a technical debate, the Committee will recognize that and you will be the one sanctioned.

### It is NOT an excuse for fragility

Receiving robust criticism of your work, or a clear "no" on a proposal, is not a CoC violation. Not having your views agreed with is not a CoC violation. Operating in a serious technical environment requires capacity for disagreement.
